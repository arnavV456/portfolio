Disaster Victim Scouting Quadcopter

A fast, lightweight quadcopter that scans large disaster-affected areas, detects victims onboard in real time, and geotags their locations for a companion heavy-payload drone to deliver medkits and supplies.

14" Glass fiber props
360KV 5010 motors
Pi + Cam Onboard detection

Overview

This quadcopter is the scouting half of a two-drone disaster response system. Built around 5010 360KV motors on 14-inch glass fiber propellers, it's tuned for speed and range over payload capacity so it can cover large search areas quickly. An onboard Raspberry Pi with a camera module runs a real-time detection model against the live feed, and confirmed victim detections are geotagged with GPS coordinates and handed off to a companion heavy-payload drone for medkit and supply delivery to those exact locations.

Mission requirements

  • Wide-area coverage at speed, prioritizing range and flight time over payload
  • Onboard, real-time victim detection without ground-station dependency
  • Accurate GPS geotagging of each detection for handoff to the delivery drone
  • Reliable operation over disaster terrain where comms and GPS may be degraded

Architecture

The Raspberry Pi runs the detection model against the camera feed in flight, and each confirmed detection is timestamped and geotagged against the flight controller's GPS fix. Rather than carrying delivery payload itself, this airframe stays light and fast purely for search — the resulting coordinate list is passed to a separate heavy-payload drone, which flies directly to the marked locations to deliver medkits or other supplies.

System role

  • Scout drone: wide-area search, onboard detection, GPS geotagging (this build)
  • Payload drone: heavy-lift delivery to marked coordinates (companion system)
  • Two airframes specialized for search and delivery respectively, rather than one drone doing both

Status

Airframe and motor/prop combo selected for search speed and endurance; onboard detection pipeline running on the Raspberry Pi with GPS geotagging of detections in progress.
