Hexacopter Power Distribution Board

A compact power distribution board for a hexacopter child drone — six ESC outputs, isolated regulated rails for a Raspberry Pi, Pixhawk Mini, and two servos, all fit inside a 75 mm diameter board.

75 mm Max diameter
40 A Per motor (6×)

Overview

Designed a power distribution board (PDB) for a hexacopter child drone in a swarm platform, sized to fit within a 75 mm diameter board. The PDB distributes raw battery power to six ESCs while providing three separate regulated low-voltage rails, isolated from each other to keep ESC and servo switching noise away from the flight controller and companion computer.

KiCadPower ElectronicsPDB Design

Power requirements

  • Six ESC outputs, up to 40 A per motor (240 A theoretical worst case)
  • 5 V / 5 A regulated rail for a Raspberry Pi
  • 5.3 V regulated, filtered rail for a Pixhawk Mini flight controller
  • 6 V / 5 A rail for two servo motors

Architecture

Raw battery voltage feeds a central distribution point that fans out to the six ESC pads directly, and to three independent buck regulator branches — one each for the Pi, Pixhawk, and servos. Keeping these rails separate prevents servo current spikes and ESC switching noise from disturbing the flight controller or companion computer.

Protection & layout strategy

  • Reverse-polarity protection via a P-channel MOSFET at the battery input
  • TVS diode across the battery rail, voltage rating matched to battery cell count
  • 2 oz copper with heavy via stitching on ESC pads and the battery connector
  • Outer ring of ESC pads for short, symmetric current paths to all six motors
  • Ferrite bead and bulk capacitance filtering the Pixhawk rail specifically
